DrawNext is a custom React web app built for the Portuguese Pavilion at Expo Osaka 2025. Participants drew on paper as part of an interactive experience at the pavilion, then used the app to photograph and upload their drawings, link them to slots in a shared digital notebook, and connect them to broader themes like artificial intelligence and the environment.

It's a lightweight, purpose-built tool — not a CMS-driven site but a bespoke application developed to support a specific cultural and diplomatic moment. The brief was precise, the timeline tight, and the result shipped for one of the most high-profile events Portugal participated in that year.

Plura built the app with a custom admin system, handling both the public-facing experience and the backend management of submissions.
