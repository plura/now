Hotel Convento de São Paulo is a 40-room hotel set in a historic convent in the Serra d'Ossa, near Redondo in the Alentejo. Some rooms occupy the original monks' cells; others are in the former stables and annexes. The property includes a restaurant, bar, banquet rooms, and cloisters — a setting that's genuinely unlike most hotel stays.

The site covers the full range of accommodation options — from standard rooms to named suites like the Viscondessa and Santa Bárbara — as well as the restaurant, events facilities, and cultural and activity offerings. It's bilingual in English and Portuguese, with a built-in booking system.

Plura built a site that lets the property speak for itself, without over-explaining the obvious appeal of staying in a 17th-century convent in the middle of the Alentejo plains.
